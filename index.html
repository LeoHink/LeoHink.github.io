Leonard Hinckeldey

PhD Candidate, University of Edinburgh, School of Informatics

L[DOT]HINCKELDEY[AT]ED.AC.UK

My research is driven by an overarching goal to train autonomous agents in simulation so that they can collaborate with any human in the real world. I currently focus on developing reinforcement learning methods that are robust when coordinating in multi-agent systems with any potential other agent, a problem setting often referred to as Ad-Hoc Teamwork or Zero-shot coordination.

I’m a second-year PhD student at the University of Edinburgh at the Centre for AI in Assistive Autonomy supervised by Prof. Subramanian Ramamoorthy. I am also a part of MARBLE (Multi-Agent, Reinforcement, Behaviour and Learning), where I am a co-organiser of the RL & Agents Reading Group. My PhD is also supported by the Honda Research Institute where I receive additional supervision from Fan Zhang and Michael Gienger.

Previously, I did a Master’s degree in Applied Social Data Science at the LSE and before that I did an undergraduate degree in Economics.
