My research is driven by an overarching goal to train autonomous agents in simulation so that they can collaborate with any human in the real world. I currently focus on developing reinforcement learning methods that are robust when coordinating in multi-agent systems with any potential other agent, a problem setting often referred to as Ad-Hoc Teamwork or Zero-shot coordination.

I'm a second-year PhD student at the University of Edinburgh at the [Centre for AI in Assistive Autonomy](https://assistive-autonomy.ed.ac.uk/) supervised by Prof. Subramanian Ramamoorthy. I am also a part of [MARBLE](https://edinburgh-rl.github.io/) (Multi-Agent, Reinforcement, Behaviour and Learning), where I am a co-organiser of the :robot: [RL & Agents Reading Group](https://edinburgh-rl.github.io/reading-group). My PhD is also supported by the [Honda Research Institute](https://www.honda-ri.de/) where I receive additional supervision from Fan Zhang and Michael Gienger.

Previously, I did a Master's degree in Applied Social Data Science at the [LSE](https://www.lse.ac.uk/) and before that I did an undergraduate degree in Economics.
