I'm a second year PhD student at the University of Edinburgh at the [Centre for AI in Assistive Autonomy](https://assistive-autonomy.ed.ac.uk/) supervised by Prof. Subramanian Ramamoorthy. My overarching goal is to train autonomous agents in simulation so that they can collaborate with any human in the real-world. 

My research focuses on developing reinforcement learning methods that are robust when coordinating in multi-agent systems with any potential other agent, a problem setting also known as Ad-Hoc Teamwork. My PhD is supported by the Honda Research Institute where I receive additional supervision from Fan Zhang and Michael Gienger.

Previously, I did a Master's degree in Applied Social Data Science, at the LSE and before that I studied Economics.
